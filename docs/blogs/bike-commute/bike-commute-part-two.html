Bike Commuting: Part Two

July 3, 2022

To the Gas Station

0.3 miles there, 0.3 miles back.

I did it! And I learned a few things.

One, my legs are not ready for a 12 mile ride a day. I wasn’t in “pain”, but it’s going to take some time for my legs to be ready for the Bike Commuting to work.

Safety

So, I road on a road that wasn’t too busy at the time of riding, but I had 5 cars pass me. That was scary. It had just recently rained, but the roads weren’t wet. There were no puddles.

Carrying Things

I was tasked with getting some chips and a sprite for my wife. How would you carry that on a bike?

I chose to carry that with a drawstring backpack. Now this is not your average drawstring backpack. This drawstring backpack has pockets! And two beverage pouches!

Things Obtained, Time to Ride

My way back was quick and safe. I took less traveled roads, and I made it back safe and sound.

Next Ride

I’m not sure when my next ride will be, but I’ll more than likely take to the track. Try to put in a few miles and see how that goes!

Eventually I’ll be doing a test ride to work, preferably on the weekend with less traffic.
