How I Review

Out of 5?

I review on a scale of 5, 1-5. I like that because it's simple and easy to understand. No half points, nothing to divide or question. 1-5.

But is that the best? Is 5 too limiting? Is a review of 5, the same as another review out of 5?

I originally chose the 1-5 review score from GoodReads.com, a book review site. I thought as I was reviewing the books that I read, or movies that I watched out of 5, so that I could write my reviews on GoodReads using their star system. I didn't want to do any math or change my score in anyway before posting my review, but do I need to change that?

What's a 4/5?

Is a 4/5 the same as an 8/10, or is it worse? My opinion, I think 8/10 is better than a 4/5. Because there is more granularity out of 10. But then why not go higher, 1-20? 1-50? 1-100?

With 1-100 you can get IN DEPTH, like for 1984 I could've given a 20/100, but at that point that's just a 1/5. So, I don't know..

So what's the best way to score?

As of right now, I'm going to stick with 1-5, but I'm going to try and be more stringent on my rating. We'll see!
